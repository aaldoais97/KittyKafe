Classic Hot Latte

The hot latte is a classic and versatile espresso drink that is so simple to make that it is often one of the very first drinks that a barista learns to make. Let's walk through how to prepare a hot latte.

A hot latte consists of two main ingredients and some optional ingredients to tweak the flavor to your liking.

Ingredients:

  • 1 part espresso (measured by volume)
  • 2 parts steamed milk
  • Sugar or sweetener to taste (optional)
  • Flavoring syrup (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the espresso puck in your portafilter. If you're a beginner barista and need help pulling a good espresso shot, here is a helpful video guide I found on YouTube.
  2. Purge your steam wand and then submerge the wand into a metal jug of milk to steam the milk. Steam until the side of the jug begins to feel too hot to touch. To get a good texture, submerge the wand at an angle and adjust so that a whirlpool forms in the milk.
  3. Once you've pulled the espresso shot and steamed the milk, simply pour the milk into the cup of espresso. And just that easily, you have a delicious latte to enjoy!
  4. Optional Step: Add sugar/sweetener and/or a flavoring syrup of your choice.
